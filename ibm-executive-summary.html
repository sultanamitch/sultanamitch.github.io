Executive Summary

IBM HR Analytics —
Employee Attrition

1,470 employees · 6 analytical dimensions · Kaggle dataset

Overview

Why do IBM employees leave?

I analyzed IBM employee attrition data to figure out why people leave. What I found was when I looked at the effort required for an IBM employee to do their job — overtime, business travel required — I saw that the more effort required from IBM employees to do their job, the higher attrition I saw. This data shows us that if IBM can lower the effort required for IBM employees to do their job, IBM can keep talent and reduce the risk of needing to replace employees and onboard new ones.

I saw that career growth has a direct impact on attrition. If IBM employees receive promotions when deserved I saw lower attrition, and when IBM employees stay at IBM longer they tend to stay employed longer, reducing attrition.

When I looked at performance ratings at IBM I noticed the data was not fully reliable — the scale runs 1 through 4, but the only recorded scores range from 3-4. This may be due to managers not being willing to hand out lower scores in fear it would reflect poorly on them. This requires more data to fully understand.

When I analyzed employee satisfaction I saw that IBM employees with higher job involvement scores had lower attrition rates — a pretty wide delta — with scores of 1 showing a 33.7% attrition rate vs 9.0% for ratings of 4.

IBM employee profiles pointed to my theory of life stage and survivorship bias — visible in marital status, job level, and age. Sales representatives at IBM have the highest attrition rate of any role at 39.8%, which is consistent with the nature of sales positions and their historically high turnover.

Recommendations

What IBM should do.

01
Audit overtime and travel requirements Employees required to work overtime or travel frequently are 3x more likely to leave. IBM should evaluate whether these demands are structurally necessary or reduceable, particularly in high-attrition roles.
02
Implement structured promotion timelines Attrition peaks for employees in the 6-10 year tenure window — a clear promotion expectation threshold. IBM should examine promotion cadence for mid-tenure employees and create clearer career pathing.
03
Improve job involvement through role ownership Job involvement showed the strongest correlation with attrition of any satisfaction variable. IBM should focus on connecting employees to meaningful work through clearer role ownership and career development.
04
Capture attrition type at separation The dataset does not distinguish voluntary resignation from involuntary termination. IBM should capture this data point at the point of separation to enable more precise future analysis.
