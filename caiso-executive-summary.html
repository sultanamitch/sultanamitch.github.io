CAISO Queue Analysis — Energy Project Screening

326 active queue projects · 4 analytical dimensions · CAISO public data

Overview

What makes an energy project deliverable?

Before a power project can connect to California's electrical grid, it has to enter CAISO's interconnection queue and work through a series of engineering studies. The outcome of that process, Full Capacity deliverability vs Energy Only has a direct impact on project viability and financing.

I pulled CAISO's public interconnection queue data and analyzed 326 active projects to understand what actually drives deliverability outcomes. The short answer: geography matters more than technology, and adding battery storage to a solar project removes downside risk without improving your odds at the top end.

Battery storage projects that were standalone projects outperform everything else in the queue. But once you're in a congested transmission region, no amount of technology mix changes that constraint.

Takeaways

What this data suggests.

  • 01
    Screen by region before screening by technology Transmission congestion is the binding constraint on Full Capacity outcomes. A project in a congested region will underperform regardless of its technology mix. Region should be the first filter in any project screening framework.
  • 02
    Battery standalone outperforms hybrid on deliverability Hybrid projects cut worst-case risk but don't improve best-case outcomes. For developers optimizing for Full Capacity status, standalone battery in a favorable region is a stronger position than hybrid in a constrained one.
  • 03
    Queue data needs a vintage filter CAISO's queue includes projects at very different stages of the study process. Deliverability outcomes for early-stage projects are preliminary and subject to change — any analysis should segment by study phase to avoid mixing confirmed and speculative outcomes.
